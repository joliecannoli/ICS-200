Dmitri Shostakovich

Dmitri Shostakovich composed ballets, operas, concertos, and 15 symphonies. He had a complex relationship with the Soviet state as he faced denunciation but gained offical favor during other periods. His legacy left behind his impactful works characterized by ambivalent tonality and sharp contrasts; it continues to be studied and performed today.

Sergei Prokofiev

Born in Ukraine, Sergei Prokofiev was one of the most recognized composers in the Modernist music movement. His works include symphonies, ballets, and orchestral suits that exhibit endless complexity and variety. He is most known for composing the Classical Symphony, the Peter and the Wolf, and the Romeo and Juliet ballet

Aram Khachaturian

Armenian born, Aram Khachaturian made a notable impact on the world of classical music while still preserving his culture by combining the rhythms of Armenian folk with formal classical compositions in Western theoretical stlyism. By doing this, he created a bridge between the east and the west, making folk themes acceptable in classical settings.
