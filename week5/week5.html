L'Inconnue de la Seine

If you've ever listened to Micheal Jackson's hit song, "Smooth Criminal", you probably have at some point
wondered who the muse for the woman named "Annie" that is mentioned in the lyrics is. You may already know
that the name "Annie," is derived from the common dummy that is used in CPR training called "Rescusci Anne",
or "Rescue Annie." When learning CPR, you are instructed to ask "Annie, are you okay?" before beginning
resuscitation; hence, the lyrics "Annie are you okay?". Annie in Smooth Criminal is inspired by a CPR
dummy, but did you know that both Annie and Rescusci Anne are both derived from a woman who is speculated
to have existed?

As the story goes: in the late 1880s, the body of a young woman was discovered in the River of Seine in
Paris. Around the time the body was discovered, many people were intrigued by how beautiful she was, but
nobody knew who this girl was, so she was then dubbed "L'Inconnue de la Seine," or in English, "The Unkown
Woman of Seine." A pathologist at the Paris Morgue was so fascinated by her well portioned face that he made
a cast of her death mask which you may recognize as the face of many CPR dummies that are used to this day.
