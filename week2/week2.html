Campfire Stories from Southeast Asia

Folklore is not only traditional and customary stories of a culture, it is also pieces of history that show aspects of what ancient humans believed as well as different cultures around the world. Here, you will read about folklores native to Southeast Asia.

Banana Tree Ghost

Banana Tree Ghost...

Known as "Nang Tani"(Lady of Tani), the banana tree ghost is native to Thailand, Laos, and Cambodia. The ghost in this folklore inhabits banana trees. In some regions, she is believed to be a benevolent spirit that gives food to buddhist monks that pass by while others believe she is a vengeful spirit who harms men, especially those that have wronged women.

Krasue

Krasue..

The origins of the krasue are not exact, but the belief in it is shared throughout Thailand, Cambodia, Laos, Vietnam, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The Krasue is believed to be a woman who appears to be a normal human during the day. During the night, her head ascends from her body with her organs attached in search for her next meal.

Mae Nak

Mae Nak...

This folklore is native to Phra Khanong in modern day Bangkok and the tale is said to have taken place during the reign of Mongkut. The legend tells of a young man, Mak, who is sent to war, leaving behind his pregnant wife, Nak. Nak and her child die due to complications during childbirth while Mak is away and when he returns home, he is welcomed by the ghost of his wife and child. Neighbors warn that Mak of the ghosts and as a result, Nak terrorizes the townspeople and whoever tries to drive Mak away from her.
